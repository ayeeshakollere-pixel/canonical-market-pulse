02The thesis: an adoption funnel
LOVE AT THE TOP → MONEY AT THE BOTTOM
How to read it

An open-source infrastructure company is a funnel. Each pillar measures one stage, and the data confirms every handoff is intact.

Top — community. Developers adopt Ubuntu for free; it leads every Linux distro at 27.8% mindshare.

Middle — cloud. That preference becomes the deployment default: >60% of public-cloud Linux is Ubuntu.

Bottom — enterprise. Organizations pay Canonical to support what their engineers already run — $292M, 83% margin.

The funnel narrows in volume but widens in value. The one crack to watch is at the very top, where developer growth has gone flat.

05So what — the executive read
Strength

Durable moat, healthy economics. Default-of-the-cloud status plus 83% gross margins is structurally strong. Canonical is #1 in the cloud-native install base and #2 in paid enterprise Linux.

Risk

Top of funnel is maturing. Flat developer growth and Android's rise are early signals enterprise demand could decelerate 2–3 years out. The metric to track quarterly.

Open question

The IPO. Profitable, ~20%/yr growth, founder-controlled. Financially IPO-capable; timing hinges on Mark Shuttleworth's intent, which public data can't resolve.
