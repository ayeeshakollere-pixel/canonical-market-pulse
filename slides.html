Market Pulse

Reading a private company's trajectory through three public signals that move before the money does.

A data-driven portfolio analysis

The challenge

Canonical has no stock ticker. It publishes no quarterly deck. A conventional "read the financials" approach fails.

So we triangulate from three independent public signals:

  • Developer adoption — What do builders choose for free?
  • Cloud market share — Does it become the deployment default?
  • Business metrics — Do organizations pay to support it?

Then validate against the one statutory filing that does exist: UK Companies House.

What this means

✓ Strength

Durable moat, healthy economics. Default-of-the-cloud status plus 83% gross margins is structurally strong. Canonical is #1 in cloud-native installs and #2 in paid enterprise Linux.

⚠ Risk

Top of funnel is maturing. Flat developer growth and Android's rise signal enterprise demand could decelerate 2–3 years out. The metric to watch quarterly.

? Open

The IPO question. Profitable, ~20%/yr growth, founder-controlled. Financially IPO-ready; timing hinges on Mark Shuttleworth's intent only.
